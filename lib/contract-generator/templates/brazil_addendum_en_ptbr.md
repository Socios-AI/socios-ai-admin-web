# BRAZIL ADDENDUM TO MASTER SOFTWARE LICENSE, COMMERCIAL PARTNERSHIP AND CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT

This Brazil Addendum applies when the Receiving Party is organized, domiciled, resident, commercially active, or materially operating in Brazil, or when Brazilian customers, users, leads, consumers, or data subjects are involved.

## 1. Language and Portuguese Materials

The English version of the Master Agreement remains controlling. Portuguese-Brazil materials may be provided for transparency, consumer information, data protection compliance, and commercial clarity. In the event of conflict, the English version controls, except where mandatory Brazilian law requires Portuguese disclosures, consumer-facing terms, privacy information, or mandatory data transfer language.

## 2. Brazilian Identity Fields

For Brazilian legal entities, the system shall render **CNPJ**, legal name, trade name, registered address, and legal representative information. For Brazilian individuals, the system shall render **CPF** and official identity information only when necessary for contract execution, compliance, payment, or enforceability.

## 3. Independent Contractor; No Employment

The Receiving Party acts independently and shall be responsible for its own staff, contractors, taxes, labor obligations, social charges, licenses, registrations, and business operations. The Receiving Party shall not create conditions that may suggest employment subordination, fixed working hours, salary, exclusivity, or operational control by the Company.

## 4. No Franchise or Commercial Representation Unless Expressly Adopted

The Parties do not intend to create a franchise, commercial representation, employment, agency, mandate, or distribution arrangement under Brazilian law unless the Company expressly adopts such model in a separate written instrument. The Receiving Party shall not present itself as franchisee, commercial representative, employee, agent, or legal representative of the Company.

## 5. LGPD and International Transfers

If personal data subject to Brazilian law is processed, the LGPD Data Processing Addendum is mandatory. The Parties shall identify roles, purposes, categories of personal data, legal bases, security measures, retention, deletion, incident notification, data subject rights, subprocessors, and international transfer mechanisms. Any ANPD standard contractual clauses used as a transfer mechanism must be incorporated as required and not modified where mandatory.

## 6. Electronic Signature

The Parties agree that electronic signatures, digital signature platforms, audit trails, timestamps, IP records, and authentication logs are valid and binding to the maximum extent permitted by applicable Brazilian law, including accepted electronic signature methods agreed by the Parties.

## 7. Marketing and Income Claims

The Receiving Party shall not make promises of guaranteed income, passive income, investment return, employment, financial security, or any earning potential not expressly approved in writing by the Company. Any partner, subpartner, or representative onboarding must be secondary to real commercialization of products or services to actual customers and must comply with the Partner Code of Conduct.

## 8. Taxes; Invoice

Payment of commissions may be conditioned on valid invoice/nota fiscal or other document required by applicable law and the Company's payment procedures. The Receiving Party is solely responsible for Brazilian taxes, accounting obligations, licenses, registrations, labor obligations, and local compliance.
